What is Tee-rific?

Tee-rific is a golf management software designed to simplify and enhance both managing and visiting a golf course. Implementing an online, comprehensive golfing application creates a convenient way for golfers to prepare for their round. Golfers possess the ability to view available tee times and rentals, engage with interactive scorecards, track their golfing history, and participate in tournaments. Since Tee-rific is heavily dependent on mobile accessibility, we prioritized a mobile-friendly experience for golfers. On the course owner’s side, we tailored the software to be practical while still retaining the customizability of pen and paper. Golf course owners can relish in the simplicity and oversight of online booking, utilizing an interactive real-time tee sheet. Tee-rific also offers private, customer-provided feedback of their course, automatic tracking of rental stock, and an extensive course biography to attract customers.

Our Process

We developed Tee-rific over six weeks, broken into three two-week iterations. To effectively gather requirements for Tee-rific, we visualized our software assisting throughout the entire golfing process. From assisting in course selection to tracking their score as they play to providing feedback after their round. Our team employed an agile environment, allowing more flexibility for taking on and completing work. As a result of Simon Fraser University campus closures due to ongoing COVID-19 restrictions, our development process was fully remote. To maintain communication and teamwork, we scheduled weekly Scrums every Tuesday and Thursday at 6:00 pm. We liaised about our recent progress, formulated plans to complete by the next meeting, and coordinated our efforts on time-consuming problems. Our testing process was in-depth and consisted of heavy cooperation between multiple team members. Upon completing a new feature, at least one fellow team member would test this feature for bugs. This technique minimized bugs and allowed for more than just the developer to understand how the code functions.
